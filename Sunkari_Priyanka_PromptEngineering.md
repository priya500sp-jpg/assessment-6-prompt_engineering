3.1 Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Option B: Multi-Step Reasoning

Problem: A company has 150 employees. 60% work in engineering, 25% in sales,
and the rest in operations. If the company plans to hire 20% more engineers
and 10% more sales people, how many total employees will there be after hiring?

Output:
Let's solve it step by step.

### 1. Current employees

Total employees = **150**

* Engineering = 60% of 150 = **0.60 × 150 = 90**
* Sales = 25% of 150 = **0.25 × 150 = 37.5**
* Operations = 150 − 90 − 37.5 = **22.5**

### 2. New hires

* Hire 20% more engineers:

  * 20% of 90 = **18**
* Hire 10% more sales people:

  * 10% of 37.5 = **3.75**

### 3. Total employees after hiring

New total = Current employees + New engineering hires + New sales hires

[
150 + 18 + 3.75 = 171.75
]

### Answer

The company will have **171.75 employees** after hiring.

**Note:** Since a company cannot have a fraction of an employee, the percentages given lead to non-whole employee counts. In a real-world scenario, employee numbers would be rounded appropriately, but mathematically the result is **171.75**.

### 4.3 Limitations & Responsible Use
